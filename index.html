Welcome

Hello, I am Hosung You, a doctoral candidate in Workforce Education and Development at Penn State University. My research focuses on human-AI collaboration, ethical leadership in digital contexts, and career development.

Currently, I am working on research projects related to agentic AI integration and ethical leadership development under the guidance of Dr. Hyung Joon Yoon. My research explores how transformational leaders can ethically integrate AI systems with human teams while preserving human agency and dignity.

A Relationship among Self-direction, Teaching Presence, Social Presence and Perceived Academic Achievement of Teachers in Distance Training Program

Kim, J. J., You, H. S.
Korean Education Inquiry, 35(3), 111-132, 2022

This research examines the relationship between self-direction, teaching presence, social presence, and perceived academic achievement in distance training programs for teachers. The study provides insights into the factors that influence effective distance education, especially in the context of teacher professional development during the COVID-19 pandemic.

Paper (in Korean)

Presentation at Korea-Japan Academic Exchange Research Conference of the Korean Society for the Study of Lifelong Education, Seoul, Republic of Korea, November 2021

This presentation explored the concept of presence in distance education during the pandemic, examining how virtual learning environments can maintain meaningful educational connections despite physical separation. The research focused on strategies for maintaining teaching presence and social presence in online lifelong education contexts.

Research Discussions

Discussion topics related to current research interests. Comments and feedback are welcome.

The Future of Work: Ethical Integration of Agentic AI in Organizations

Posted: May 15, 2025 | Comments: 0

As AI systems become increasingly autonomous, how should organizations prepare for the integration of "AI colleagues"? This discussion explores the ethical dimensions of human-AI teaming, focusing on how Branson's Structured Self-Reflection Model can help leaders develop the ethical acumen needed to navigate complex questions of trust, accountability, and human agency in AI-augmented workplaces.

Phenomenological Experiences of AI Adoption: Navigating Employee Anxiety and Adaptation

Posted: May 1, 2025 | Comments: 0

How do employees subjectively experience the integration of AI systems into their work? This discussion examines the emotional responses, resistance patterns, and adaptation strategies that emerge when organizations adopt increasingly sophisticated AI tools. I'm particularly interested in how workforce education professionals can better support employees through this transition while maintaining psychological safety and well-being.

Developing Competency Models for Human-AI Collaboration

Posted: April 18, 2025 | Comments: 0

What competencies are essential for effective collaboration with AI systems? Traditional competency models need to evolve to address the unique challenges of working alongside intelligent technologies. This discussion explores frameworks for identifying, developing, and assessing the skills needed in an AI-augmented workplace, with special attention to balancing technical proficiency with distinctly human capabilities like ethical judgment and creativity.
