Publications and Presentations

A Relationship among Self-direction, Teaching Presence, Social Presence and Perceived Academic Achievement of Teachers in Distance Training Program

Kim, J. J., You, H. S.
Korean Education Inquiry, 35(3), 111-132, 2022

This research examines the relationship between self-direction, teaching presence, social presence, and perceived academic achievement in distance training programs for teachers. The study provides insights into the factors that influence effective distance education, especially in the context of teacher professional development during the COVID-19 pandemic.

Paper (in Korean)

A presence in distance lifelong education in a pandemic situation

Kim, J. J., & You, H. S.
Presentation at Korea-Japan Academic Exchange Research Conference of the Korean Society for the Study of Lifelong Education, Seoul, Republic of Korea, November 2021

This presentation explored the concept of presence in distance education during the pandemic, examining how virtual learning environments can maintain meaningful educational connections despite physical separation. The research focused on strategies for maintaining teaching presence and social presence in online lifelong education contexts.

Presentation Materials (Available Upon Request)

How do transformational leaders promote work engagement?: A meta-analytic structural equation modeling (MASEM) approach

You, H. S.
Master's Thesis, Korea University, 2022

This master's thesis investigated the relationship between transformational leadership and work engagement through a meta-analytic structural equation modeling approach. The research synthesized existing studies to understand the mechanisms and pathways through which transformational leaders influence employee engagement in the workplace.

Thesis Abstract (Available Upon Request)

A study on exploring performance of policy research in employment and future improvement through Meta-analysis

Ministry of Employment and Labor & Korea University Research Team
Project funded by the Ministry of Employment and Labor, Republic of Korea, 2020

As part of a research team, this project examined the performance factors of policy research in the employment field. The study used quantitative meta-analysis and expert focus group interviews to identify trends in domestic employment policies and derive future policy research tasks.

The Role of Transformational Leadership in Post-Pandemic Work Environments

Posted: May 5, 2024 | Comments: 0

As organizations continue to navigate the post-pandemic landscape, transformational leadership has emerged as a crucial factor in maintaining employee engagement and productivity. This discussion explores how transformational leadership practices can be adapted to hybrid and remote work environments, and what implications this has for HRD professionals and organizational leaders.

Meta-Analysis in HRD Research: Challenges and Opportunities

Posted: March 28, 2024 | Comments: 0

Meta-analysis offers powerful tools for synthesizing research findings in human resource development. This discussion explores methodological considerations in conducting meta-analyses in HRD research, including challenges in study selection, coding, and addressing publication bias, as well as opportunities for advancing theory and practice through meta-analytic approaches.
